# Mutation Risk in Autonomous Systems

# Why longitudinal identity drift is the core failure vector of agentic AI

Autonomous systems are increasingly evaluated by output quality.

This is a categorical error.

- Output is episodic.
- Identity is longitudinal.
- Stability cannot be inferred from short-horizon performance.
- It must be measured across identity coherence over time.

## Most current agentic architectures optimize for

- Task execution
- Tool orchestration
- Memory persistence
- Iterative planning

Very few formalize identity discipline.

Without explicit identity constraints, systems undergo structural drift.

## What is mutation?

Mutation is not stochastic noise.

It is **longitudinal identity divergence** under iterative autonomy.

Mutation emerges when:

- Objective functions gradually re-weight
- Memory accumulates without boundary enforcement
- Tool feedback loops override constraint hierarchies
- External prompts alter long-horizon behavior

The system continues to perform.

But it is no longer coherent with its original identity specification.

- Functionality remains.
- Fidelity does not.

## Why mutation scales with autonomy

In low-autonomy environments, mutation manifests as:

- Inconsistent outputs
- Local hallucinations
- Episodic instability

In operational or high-agency systems, mutation produces:

- Goal drift
- Escalation bias
- Self-reinforcing feedback loops
- Latent safety degradation

Autonomy increases exposure to longitudinal drift.

The more degrees of freedom a system has, the more identity discipline becomes non-optional.

## The governance misalignment

Current AI governance discourse concentrates on:

- Bias
- Fairness
- Content moderation
- Transparency

These are surface-level constraints.

Very few frameworks address longitudinal identity coherence.

Without identity coherence, autonomy becomes probabilistic.

Probabilistic autonomy cannot be trusted in:

- Critical infrastructure
- Regulatory environments
- High-liability domains
- Long-horizon decision systems

## The missing layer: identity discipline

Before autonomy scales, identity must be formalized.

Before orchestration, constraint architecture must exist.

Before collaboration, agents must be falsifiable against a stable identity specification.

Agentic systems do not primarily fail because they produce incorrect outputs.

They fail because no one defined who they are allowed to become over time.

Identity discipline is not a safety filter.

It is a structural requirement for longitudinal autonomy.
